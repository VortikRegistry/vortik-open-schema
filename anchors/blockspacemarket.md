**Registry ID:** `blockspacemarket`

**Classification:** deprecated  

---

## Summary

This anchor tracks **blockspace markets** as a legacy abstraction used to describe blockspace as an economic coordination surface.

While historically useful as a broad framing, the term has become less precise as Ethereum coordination has increasingly shifted toward protocol-native primitives, roles, commitments, payloads, and constraints.

In the current registry, this anchor is preserved as a deprecated semantic surface rather than as a canonical protocol-facing term.

---

## Context

The idea of a **blockspace market** emerged as a way to describe how access to block inclusion, transaction ordering, and execution capacity could be understood through supply, demand, fees, and competition.

This framing was useful during earlier phases of Ethereum discourse because it captured the economic pressure around transaction inclusion and execution access.

However, as Ethereum research and implementation language evolved, this broad market framing became less precise than newer protocol-facing terminology tied to:

- builders
- proposers
- bids
- payloads
- commitments
- inclusion constraints
- block access lists
- protocol-native execution pathways

As a result, **blockspace markets** remains understandable as a historical and ecosystem-level narrative, but it no longer maps cleanly to a single canonical coordination primitive.

Legacy Economic Framing / Broad Coordination Abstraction

## Coordination Role

Blockspace markets do not define a specific protocol role, primitive, or constraint.

Instead, the term acts as a broad economic abstraction describing competition over access to:

- block inclusion
- execution capacity
- transaction ordering
- MEV opportunities
- scarce settlement resources

Its weakness is that it compresses multiple distinct coordination surfaces into a single market narrative, reducing semantic precision.

---

## Structural Importance

This anchor remains useful for documenting historical and ecosystem-level discourse.

However, its structural precision is lower than newer protocol-native surfaces.

In modern Ethereum coordination language, more precise terms increasingly replace this abstraction when discussing:

- builder behavior
- proposer-builder separation
- inclusion guarantees
- payload construction
- execution constraints
- block access lists
- commitment-based coordination

This makes blockspace markets more useful as a legacy conceptual frame than as a current canonical anchor.
